Rotamaster Witold Pilecki

Voluntary prisoner of the KL Auschwitz

Officer of the Polish Army reserves, cofounder of the Polish Secret Army, voluntary prisoner of the KL Auschwitz, officer of the Main Headquarters of the Home Army and 'NIE', political prisoner of the Stalinist period, the victim of a judiciary murder.

TIMELINE

  • 1901 - he was born in the area of Nowogródczyzna, Poland.
  • 1910 - his mother Ludwika and his brothers and sisters moved to Vilnus, where he joined the secret ZHP Scouts organization.
  • 1918 - during World War I, Pilecki joined Polish self-defense units in the Wilno area with which he helped collect weapons and disarm retreating German troops in what became the prelude to the Vilna offensive.
  • 1919 - 1920 - during the Polish-Soviet War, Pilecki commanded a ZHP Scout section that was overrun by the Bolsheviks. He later joined the regular Polish Army and fought in the Polish retreat from Kiev.
  • 1920 - on August 5, 1920, Pilecki joined the 211th Uhlan Regiment and fought in the Battle of Warsaw and at Rudniki Forest and took part in the liberation of Wilno.
  • 1940 - during World War II, Pilecki smuggled himself into Auschwitz under the false name Tomasz Serafinski and began recruiting members for an underground resistance group that he organized into a coherent movement. He began sending information about what was going on inside the camp and confirming that the Nazis were seeking the extermination of the Jews to Britain and the United States as early as 1941. Pilecki used a courier system that the Polish Resistance operated throughout occupied Europe to channel the reports to the Allies.
  • 1942 - Pilecki's resistance group had learned of the existence of the gas chambers and began work on several plans to liberate Auschwitz, including one in which the RAF would bomb the walls or Free Polish paratroopers would fly in from Britain.
  • 1943 - he escaped with two other prisoners after he voluntarily spent 2½ years at the camp smuggling out its darkest secrets to the Allies.
  • 1944 - 1944, Pilecki was captured while fighting in the Warsaw Uprising in 1944 and spent the rest of the war in a prisoner-of-war camp.
  • 1945 - he joined the Free Polish troops in Italy and agreed to return to Poland and gather intelligence on its takeover by the Soviets.
  • 1947 - Pilecki was caught by the Polish Communist regime, tortured, interrogated on his espionage.
  • 1948 - Pilecki was executed on May 25, 1948 at Warsaw's Mokotow Prison, following a trial at which he was given three death sentences..
"I've been trying to live my life so that in the hour of my death I would rather feel joy, than fear."

Rotamaster Witold Pilecki
